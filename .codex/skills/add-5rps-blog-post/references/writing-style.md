# Sho writing style for 5RPS updates

Use this guide after reading the current website posts and the input folder. The production-update rules in `AGENTS.md` take priority where an older article habit conflicts with the film site.

## Voice fingerprint

- Start from the real event, artifact, test, or problem.
- Explain technical or production work as if speaking to a colleague who understands the field but did not see this week's work.
- Use specific nouns naturally. Name the tool, stage, shot count, version, date, location, or constraint when the source supports it.
- Move between a concrete example and the larger workflow.
- Allow occasional short questions or fragments when they sound natural.
- Use first person only for a genuinely signed creator account.
- Keep some warmth and opinion, but do not manufacture drama.
- Prefer practical next steps over motivational closing language.

The reference articles use talk context, named systems such as Google Cloud, Vertex AI, Qdrant, Jupyter Notebook, Flask, and Kubernetes, numerical experiment results, small narrative examples, and incremental process explanations. Carry those structural traits into production writing. Do not copy their wording or force technical-blog conventions into a short film update.

## Sentence and paragraph rhythm

- Mix sentence lengths. Aim for roughly 20 to 30 percent under 10 words when it occurs naturally.
- Vary paragraph length. A one-line observation may sit beside a four-sentence explanation.
- Use contractions in English where Sho would say them aloud.
- Connect paragraphs through subject matter, not canned transitions.
- Use lists for actual inventories, stages, results, or questions. Do not turn ordinary prose into a three-item marketing list.
- Use headings only when they help readers navigate a substantial post.

## Hard bans

Do not use em dashes in authored EN or JA prose. Hyphens inside established compounds and source identifiers are allowed.

Never use these phrases or close formulaic variants:

- `It is important to note that`
- `But here's the catch`
- `This underscores the importance of`
- `It cannot be denied that`
- `As of my knowledge cutoff`
- `In today's fast-paced world`
- `In this ever-evolving landscape`
- `In the digital age`
- `In conclusion`
- `To summarize`
- `Let's delve into`
- `delve deeper`
- `At its core`
- `at the core`
- `game-changer`
- `uncharted waters`
- `embark on a journey`
- `treasure trove of information`

Avoid contrast templates such as:

- `X isn't the problem, Y is`
- `not just X, but Y`
- `They don't need X, they need Y`
- `X aren't Y. They're Z.`
- a rhetorical question followed by a dash and a slogan-like answer

Do not announce a generic introduction, body, recap, or conclusion. Do not use `Moreover`, `Furthermore`, or `Additionally` more than once total in an 800-word post. Avoid consecutive paragraphs beginning with `However` or `Therefore`.

## Restricted language

Across one English post, use no more than two total occurrences from this list unless a term is part of a factual proper name or direct source quotation:

`leverage`, `optimize`, `enhance`, `utilize`, `synergy`, `deliverables`, `holistic`, `capability`, `pivotal`, `crucial`, `groundbreaking`, `cutting-edge`, `explore`, `delve`, `ensure`, `foster`, `embark`, `significant`, `relevant`, `dynamic`, `innovative`, `comprehensive`, `robust`, `streamlined`

Usually replace them with the actual action: `upload`, `compare`, `draw`, `test`, `cut`, `render`, `review`, `publish`, or `reduce`.

## Evidence rules

- Attach every concrete claim to something in the provided folder, current repository, or an explicitly approved source.
- Name the source for statistics and trends. Omit unsupported numbers rather than estimating.
- Preserve uncertainty from the source material.
- Do not invent reader quotes, production obstacles, emotional reactions, or insider detail.
- Proper nouns and concrete examples improve a post only when they are real. Do not meet a numeric quota by padding.

## English check

- Does the first paragraph say what happened?
- Can a reader picture one real artifact or production action?
- Are technical terms explained only as much as this audience needs?
- Does each heading earn its place?
- Does the final paragraph state a real next step or end on the last useful fact?
- Can any sentence be deleted without losing meaning?

## Japanese check

- Keep the public production voice collective. Omit the subject where Japanese remains clear; use `私たち` sparingly and never reveal or imply team size.
- Rebuild each paragraph around natural Japanese rhythm instead of matching English sentence boundaries.
- Preserve technical density, candidness, and dry humor. Do not add explanations merely because the source is English.
- Prefer established production terms: `脚本`, `絵コンテ`, `アニマティック`, `演出`, `リポジトリ`, `レビューアプリ`, and `エージェント`.
- Keep headings short, concrete, and idiomatic rather than translating English headings mechanically.
- The final line must be the canonical Discord invitation using `https://discord.gg/cWae4TfR`.
- Does the post read as Japanese written for a production audience rather than translated English?
- Does it announce the completed action before commentary?
- Does it use precise production vocabulary and `です・ます` consistently?
- Are subject omissions natural and unambiguous?
- Are short titles and semantic units protected without applying no-wrap to prose?
- Are promotional loanwords and abstract claims removed?
